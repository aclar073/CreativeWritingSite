Sensory Description

Advice

The sensory details you select in your writing should create for your reader the same picture you have in your mind. Instead of using vague, general words, your sensory language should be concrete and sensory-packed. This makes the difference between vivid and vague language.

IUP Writing Center

Sample 1

Her father is sitting in his recliner in front of the television. His open eyes are glassy and transfixed towards the ceiling. What at first appeared to be a blanket covering him, comes into focus as a swarm of flies. They begin to buzz around the room following the disturbance with the plate. One of the flies landed briefly in his eye and Jamie felt her stomach turn. It was then she saw the dried blood crusted on his favorite jersey. His large stomach protrudes from the strained fabric . The odor is unbearable. She can taste the smell of decay and resists the urge to vomit. Slowly, as if detached from her body, she closes the door. A single tear streaks down her face and she quickly wipes it away with her sleeve.

- Excerpt from Home for the Holidays

This sample text addresses many of the senses including sight, sound, smell, and taste. This allowed me to create a realistic and equally horrific scene.

Sample 2

During the day, the house is quite unassuming. The shutters hang haphazardly from the darkened windows. The porch sags in the middle as if tired of remaining upright. There is paint peeling from every exterior surface. Neighbors passed by without a second glance at the overgrown yard riddled with weeds, thankful that their properties appeared nicer by comparison.

- Excerpt from Untitled

This sample only addresses the sense of sight but does so in a very descriptive way in an attempt to assist readers in visualizing the neglected house.

Sample 3

This stranger was indeed strange. He carried a battered leather briefcase that looked as if it were new sometime in the 1950’s. In fact, everything about this man appeared to come from the 1950’s. He wore a pair of charcoal gray high waisted trousers, a white dress shirt, and a matching charcoal gray topcoat. His light brown hair was parted to the side and held in place with some form of gel. To Mr. Jones, it appeared as if this man stepped out of the past and landed on his front porch. As he performed his visual inspection of the stranger, he was meet with the confident grin of a businessman.

- Excerpt from Untitled

The sample describes the physical appearance of an uninvited guest. I enjoyed researching 1950's businessman fashion and tried to include as much detail from my research as possible.
