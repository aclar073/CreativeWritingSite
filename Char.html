Characterization

Advice

Controlling character likability is very important, as it ensures you are writing diverse characters and conveying conflicting forces. One way to manipulate likability is through narrative distance, the proximity between characters and readers. Much like a camera, you can zoom into a character's point of view to create closeness between characters (subjects) and readers (viewers), or you can zoom out to create distance. Just as adjusting the camera's focus allows you to change the physical distance between the subject and the viewer, adjusting the narrative's focus allows you to change the emotional and psychological distances between the character and the reader.

Writer's Digest

Sample 1

Jamie was not excited about the upcoming family trip. She had spent the past twelve years working odd jobs to pay her tuition, meeting the man of her dreams, and advancing her career as a publicist. She worked hard. She was approaching a promotion. She and her husband had two beautiful little boys. Life was perfect and she had made it that way. The thought of returning to that home filled her with dread.

- Excerpt from Home for the Holidays

This sample text shows my attempt at creating a backstory for the main character Jamie.

Sample 2

Performing his daily ritual, Hugh reaches down and shuffles through the pile of unwashed clothes. His hand hits the hardcover of a book. He grabs the book, straightens, and lets out another yawn. He doesn't have to search for the page anymore. He quickly flips past the images of his peers, many of them scratched out with dark black Xs. He does not miss them.

Then he sees her. Christie Halloway in her prime. She is smiling at the camera, her straight blonde hair blowing in the wind. She is precariously perched atop a pyramid of other girls with her arms held towards the sky. He remembers that night clearly, it was autumn and he had sat in the stands facing the cheerleaders. He was bundled in a thick winter coat. He remembers wondering if she was cold. Her bare legs exposed to the elements in her uniform. It left very little to the imagination and he quite enjoyed the view. Still, deep in the memory, Hugh absently rubs his thumb over the image. He misses her dearly.

- Excerpt from title

In this story, I was attempting to make an unlikable main character. This excerpt shows his unhealthy obsession with a girl from high school.
