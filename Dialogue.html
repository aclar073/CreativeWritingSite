Dialogue

Advice

1. Make it concise. The fewer words used to make a point, the better. Go back and cut, cut, cut—to the bone—keeping only those lines that convey character and thus entertain.

2. Include subtext. What people say and what they really mean are often different things, with meaning often buried under the lines, and not floating on them.

3. Be illogical. People are, especially when they speak—especially when they argue. For this reason dialogue shouldn't always make sense. Nor do people listen when others speak; even if they do, they often don't respond directly to what's just been said. When writing dialogue, try, every three or four lines at least, to deal from the bottom of the deck, to have characters say something surprising if not bizarre, something at least not obvious.

Writer's Digest

Sample 1

"It will be two nights hon. Time will fly by, and we'll be on our way back home before you know it." Richard said lightly, stroking her back. She curled against his neck, her tangled brown curls hiding her wet eyes.

“Your mom seemed excited to meet the kids.” He added softly. Jamie grunted in reply but said nothing.

"Come to bed. You'll feel worse if you're running on no sleep." He said giving her a tight squeeze around the shoulders. She nodded and let him lead her upstairs.

- Excerpt from Home for the Holidays

Sample 2

“What are you trying to sell me?” Mr. Jones asked curtly, hoping his disapproval was evident in his gravelly voice.

“Why, Mr. Jones, I am not trying to see you anything. I have a proposal for you, free of charge, I think you may appreciate.”

How does this kook know my name?

Mr. Jones thought to himself. He was caught off guard by this and simply grunted at the man. He considered closing the door and ending the conversation then.

- Excerpt from Untitled

Sample 3

"Christie," He tries again, "I thought you were gone like the others. I thought I would never see you again." He feels tears moisten his ruddy cheeks. He fights back the tears but a sniffle escapes him.

Christie shifts her weight and glances towards the front doors of the store. She says nothing.

"I've missed you so much, Christie. I've thought about you every day. I loo – "

"Listen, Hubert," She interrupts his monologue, "I know I said maybe if you were the last man on earth, but…" She leaves the sentence unfinished, allowing Hugh to figure out the rest.

- Excerpt from 76 Days of Solitude
