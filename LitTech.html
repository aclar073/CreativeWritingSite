Literary Techniques with Definitions

Characterization

Characterization is the representation of the traits, movtive, and psychology of a character in a narrative. Characterization may occur through direct description, in which the character's qualities are described by a narrator, another character, or by the chracter him or herself. It may also occur indirectly, in which the character's qualities are revealed by his or her actions, thoughts, or dialogue.

Plot

Plot is the sequence of interconnected events within the story of a play, novel, film, epic, or other narrative literary work. More than simply an account of what happened, plot reveals the cause-and-effect relationships between the events that occur.

Dialogue

Dialogue is the exchange of spoken words between two or more characters in a book, play, or other written work.

Sensory Description/Imagery

Imagery, in any sort of writing, refers to descriptive language that engages the human senses.

Point of View

Point of view refers to the perspective that the narrator holds in relation to the events of the story. The three primary points of view are first person, in which the narrator tells a story from their own perspective; second person, in which the narrator tells a story about you, the reader or viewer; and third person, in which the narrator tells a story about other people.
